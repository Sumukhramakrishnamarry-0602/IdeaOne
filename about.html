Founder Story

Built from two disciplines that aren't supposed to mix.

Sumukh Ramakrishna Marry is a first-year university student running a dual focus in Business Administration and Computer Science — not as a hedge, but as the actual architecture of how IDEAONE operates. The CS side means he writes the systems himself: object-oriented logic in C++ and Java, automation scripts that remove manual work from a creator's backend, and data pipelines that track what's actually converting instead of what's just getting views. The BBA side means none of that code exists in a vacuum — every system is built against a business mechanic: acquisition cost, retention, time-to-output, infrastructure capacity.

"I didn't want to start an agency that resells content advice. I wanted to build the backend first — the automation, the data layer, the actual engineering — and let the growth work sit on top of something solid."

Before IDEAONE, that meant building combat sports analytics tools as a research project — structured data pulled from chaotic, unstructured environments. The same instinct applies here: take a creator's scattered content output and turn it into a structured, trackable, repeatable system. IDEAONE exists because that bridge — between clean code and high-level business mechanics — was missing for creators who needed both and could only ever hire for one.
